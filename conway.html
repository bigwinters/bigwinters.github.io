Conway's Game of Life

John Conway invented The Game of Life in 1970, following his interest in a previous mathematician's research into machineâ€™s being able to create copies of themselves. The rules are simple:

  • Any live cell with one neighbour or less dies through underpopulation.
  • If a live cell has more than three neighbours it perishes through overpopulation
  • Live cells need either two or three neighbours to survive to the next generation
  • If an empty cell is surrounded by exactly three live cells, life occurs.
